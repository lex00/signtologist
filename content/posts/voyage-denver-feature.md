<h1 style="text-align: center;">Great way to start 2020 with a little bit of press! Thanks to Voyage Denver for the opportunity to tell my story.</h1>
<p>Today we’d like to introduce you to Dan Ericson.<br />
Dan, please share your story with us. How did you get to where you are today?<br />
I’m a 4th generation Colorado native. I grew up in south Denver and spent most weekends at my Grandma’s house in Swansea. My mother who is also an artist introduced me to art at a young age. Throughout school, I always gravitated towards art as my creative outlet. After graduating from South High School I attended the Colorado Art Institute. I obtained a degree in Animation and started doing freelance design for local bands. I also started producing a weekly comic strip with my best friend from South High. I didn’t get paid for the comic strip or any of the design work I was doing at the time. I enjoyed seeing my designs on concert flyers posted up and down Broadway. I felt accomplished having a weekly published comic strip that ended up running for six years and won a few awards.<br />
But I knew there was more I was capable of as an artist. I ended up going back to school because what I was taught in 96-98 at art school had already become outdated. While attending community college to brush up on my graphic design skills. I stumbled upon this medium after ruining a piece for a project due the next day. I had a no parking sign on hand and used it out of desperation to finish my assignment. 20 years later I’m still creating artwork on street signs.</p>
